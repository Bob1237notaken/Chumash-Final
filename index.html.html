Grammar & Skills

Prefixes, suffixes, and trop marks — the tools to decode any pasuk.

Prefixes
הֲ

Hey HaShe'eilah

Appears at the start of a word/sentence and turns it into a yes/no question.

הֶחָזָק הוּאIs it strong?
הֲרַפֶהIs it weak?
הֲרַק אַךְ בְּמֹשֶׁהIs it only through Moshe?
בְּ

Prefix Bet

  • In / At — location or time
    בְּמִדְבַּר סִינַיIn the Wilderness of Sinai
  • Against — opposition
    וַתְּדַבֵּר מִרְיָם בְּמֹשֶׁהMiriam spoke against Moshe
כִּי

Ki — Three Meanings

Always use context clues to determine which meaning applies:

  • Because
  • If
  • When
אֲשֶׁר

Asher — Connector

Connects ideas and adds description.

  • Means which or that
  • Links a noun to extra detail

Trop Marks (טַעֲמֵי הַמִּקְרָא)

These tell you how to group words into ideas — not just how to sing!

1
מֶרְכָא טִפְחָא
The Connector
Connects words together into one phrase — read as a single idea, like "yellow bus."
2
אֶתְנַחְתָּא
The Big Break
The main pause in the middle of a verse. Splits the pasuk into its two main parts. Big pause!
3
זָקֵף קָטֹון
The Small Pause
A smaller pause than אֶתְנַחְתָּא — a brief break, not the end of a major section.
4
סוֹף פָּסוּק
End of Verse
The "stop sign" at the very end of a פָּסוּק.

Essential Torah Rules

Three big principles Rashi uses to explain the Torah.

סְמִיכוּת פָּרָשִׁיּוֹת
Juxtaposition
The Torah places two sections next to each other on purpose — to teach a specific lesson. Even if the events happened at different times, the placement conveys meaning.
Bamidbar 8:2 — The lighting of the Menorah follows the offerings of the Nesi'im → Aharon felt left out, so Hashem comforted him with the Menorah mitzvah.
Bamidbar 9:1 — Pesach Sheni appears here even though it happened earlier → Rashi explains the Torah chose this placement intentionally.
Bamidbar 13:2 — The Spies story follows Miriam's sin → The spies saw Miriam punished for lashon hara yet failed to learn from it.
אֵין מוּקְדָם וּמְאוּחָר בַּתּוֹרָה
The Torah Is Not in Chronological Order
The Torah is organized to teach themes and ideas, not strictly by when events happened. The order of the text teaches lessons beyond just history.
Bamidbar 9:1 — Pesach Sheni (14 Nissan, Year 1) appears after events from the 2nd year. The Torah placed it here for a reason beyond timeline.
גְּזֵרָה שָׁוָה
Shared Phrase — Learning Between Verses
When two verses share the same exact phrase or word, we learn information from one verse and apply it to the other.
Example: The Torah says the עֵדָה gathered at the אֹהֶל מוֹעֵד — we learn the נְשִׂיאִים (leaders) did as well, because they share the same phrase.
